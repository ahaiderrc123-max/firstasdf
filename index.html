Concept 01 — Negative Externalities

The Hidden Bill
Society Pays

When a factory burns fossil fuels, its profit ignores the pollution costs borne by everyone else. This gap between private cost and social cost is a market failure at the heart of climate change.

Concept 03 — Tragedy of the Commons

The Shared Lake
Nobody Owns

How Much Is
Tomorrow Worth?

The discount rate determines how we value future costs vs. present costs. A high rate says future generations matter little; a low rate demands action now.

Stern Review (2006): Used a near-zero discount rate (1.4%), concluding climate action costs ~1% of GDP now vs. 5–20% in damages if ignored. Critics argued it overweighted the future. This debate shapes all climate policy.
